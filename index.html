About Me

  • Next

I am a first-year PhD student at the University of Wisconsin - Madison studying Machine Learning.

At UW-Madison, I've taken a Machine Learning and Matrix Methods course while being a TA for the Intro to Programming course (taught in Java). I've also been working on an independent study with Professor Somesh Jha investigating the suceptability of deep neural networks to membership inference attacks

I am originally from Massachusetts and attended Carleton College in Minnesota. While at Carleton, I served as my class's representative in the student government, worked as a teaching assistant for the Programming Languages course, and conducted three research projects. In my free time, you can find me playing golf, cooking, or bouldering.

University Of Wisconsin - Madison: Independent Study

University of Wisconsin - Madison w/ Dr. Somesh Jha
Sept. 2021-Curr.

Performed membership inference attacks on convolutional neural networks to reconstruct the models’ training set using a generative adversarial network.

Carleton College Diabetes Data Science: CS Researcher

Carleton College w/ Dr. Dave Musicant
Jun. 2019-Jun. 2021

\item Conducted exploratory research investigating the effects of menstrual cycle hormones on insulin sensitivity in individuals with type I diabetes by analyzing blood glucose and insulin time series data. Accounted for variable schedules by utilizing dynamic time warping while performing clustering in order to identify average day series for each individual.

Ottawa Hospital Research Institute: CS Research Intern

Ottawa Hospital Research Institute w/ Dr. Theodore Perkins
Jun. 2020-Aug. 2020

Predicted localized gene expression within tissue samples by training a convolutional neural network on H\&E slide images and testing against spatial transcriptomics data. Achieved a maximum percentage variance explained of 20.57 when predicting expression of the 10 most highly expressed genes in a mouse main olfactory bulb dataset.
